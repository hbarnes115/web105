Academic Achiever

Hannah Barnes was a consistently successful student. Educated through an exclusive private school and in-home education, Hannah graduated at the top of her class and was honored as the Class of 2023 valedictorian. While in school, Hannah achieved gifted level in English, and was consistently ahead of her grade level in history, economics, and writing. She graduated cum laude, with almost eight years of consistent community service, accumulating 724 hours of volunteering time. Hannah received an ACT composite score of 31, with an English score of 35, one point below the highest possible score.
While a student, Hannah sang with a college chorus, and participated in the Crystal Lake Raue Center for the Arts’ annual Sing Along Handel’s Messiah. She competed in 4-H Hippology and Horse Bowl, an equestrian quiz team, and represented McHenry County at the state competition for Hippology twice.
When Hannah enrolled at McHenry County College, she was recruited on two separate occasions for the competitive speech team, but turned the offers down in order to continue her volunteering. She plans to graduate with the Silver Cord Award for Community Service.
Hannah continues to volunteer in multiple capacities, serving in her church choir and on an event organization team. Outside of her volunteering, Hannah works in customer service as well as working as a note taker on her college campus. Hannah is the operator of an Etsy shop, with her first collection launching this fall. She also intends to use her private education to teach a private etiquette class.
